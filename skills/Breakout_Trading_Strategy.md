## Core Concept

A **breakout** occurs when price moves beyond a defined resistance or support level with increased volume. The goal: catch the beginning of a new trend after a period of consolidation.

**Key insight from altFINS:** *"Higher-conviction setups come from RSI divergence + breakout confirmation."*

---

## Types of Breakouts

### 1. Resistance Breakout (Bullish)
- Price consolidates below a horizontal resistance
- Breaks above resistance on increased volume
- **Trade:** Buy on breakout, sell at next resistance

### 2. Support Breakout (Bearish)
- Price consolidates above horizontal support
- Falls below support on volume
- **Trade:** Sell on breakdown, cover at next support

### 3. Range Breakout
- Price confined in a trading range (resistance + support)
- Breakout in either direction
- **Trade:** Follow the direction of breakout

### 4. Wedge/Triangle Breakout
- Price compressing into a narrowing pattern
- Usually breaks in direction of prior trend
- **Trade:** Enter when price breaks the trendline of the wedge

### 5. Moving Average Breakout
- Price crosses above/below major MA (50, 100, 200)
- Confirmed by volume
- **Trade:** Follow the crossover direction

## Entry Rules (High Probability Setup)

```
PRIMARY SETUP:
1. Consolidation phase: Price stuck between support and resistance
2. Volume contracting during consolidation (low volatility)
3. Volume EXPANDING on breakout (at least 2x 20-period average)
4. Price closes decisively beyond the level (not just touching)
5. RSI confirms momentum (RSI > 50 for longs, < 50 for shorts)

CONFIRMATION CHECKLIST (ALL must be YES):
□ Volume spike on breakout (2x+ average)
□ Price closes beyond resistance (not just intraday spike)
□ RSI confirming direction
□ No major news/catalyst conflict
□ Risk:Reward minimum 1:1.5
```

---

## Stop Loss Placement

| Breakout Type | Stop Loss Location |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Resistance break (long) | Just below the broken resistance |
| Support break (short) | Just above the broken support |
| Wedge break | Other side of the wedge pattern |
| MA break | Other side of the MA |

**Rule:** Stop loss should be just outside the consolidation zone, not inside it.

---

## Take Profit Targets

### Method 1: Measured Move
```
Target = Breakout Price + (Range Height × 1.0-1.5)

If SOL traded $80-$85 range (range = $5):
  Breakout above $85
  Target = $85 + ($5 × 1.5) = $92.50
```

### Method 2: Next Resistance
- Sell when price reaches the next major resistance level
- Works well in trending markets

### Method 3: Trailing Stop
- Once in profit, trail stop behind price
- Move to breakeven when 2x risk achieved
- Then trail by 50% of remaining move

## Volume Confirmation Rules

**Critical:** Without volume, breakouts are often FALSE.

| Volume Signal | Interpretation |
|---------------|----------------|
| Volume expanding on breakout | **CONFIRMED** — likely real move |
| Volume declining on breakout | **SUSPICIOUS** — likely false breakout |
| Volume spike before breakout | **ACCUMULATION** — strong signal |
| Volume declining during consolidation | **Coiling** — explosive move coming |

**The 2x Rule:** Volume on breakout should be at least 2x the 20-period average volume.

---

## RSI Divergence as Confirmation

**Highest conviction setup:**

1. Price approaching resistance, making lower highs (weakening)
2. RSI also making lower highs (bearish divergence forming)
3. BUT price breaks through resistance anyway (strong hands buying)
4. → **This is a powerful bullish signal** (smart money buying despite weakness)

**Why it works:** Price made a "false" show of weakness, but smart money was accumulating. The breakout is more powerful for having fooled the crowd.

## False Breakout Identification

### Signs of False Breakout:
- Price spikes beyond level but immediately reverses
- Volume decreases on the breakout attempt
- Price cannot hold the new level for more than a few candles
- RSI divergence going against the breakout direction

### Prevention:
```
1. WAIT for candle close beyond the level (not just a wick)
2. WAIT for retest of the broken level (becomes support for longs)
3. Use limit orders slightly BELOW resistance for buys (better fill)
4. Never chase — if you miss the breakout, wait for pullback
```

## Crypto-Specific Considerations

### Bitcoin Halving Cycles
- Historically, major breakouts occur 12-18 months post-halving
- Use on higher timeframes (daily, weekly)

### Altcoin Breakouts
- Altcoin breakouts often fail in bear markets
- Volume matters MORE for alts (lighter liquidity)
- Smaller market cap = more volatile breakouts

### Best Timeframes for Crypto
- **4H:** Best for swing trades (2-5 days)
- **Daily:** For position trades (1-4 weeks)
- **1H:** For scalping (intraday)
- Avoid sub-1H for breakout trades (too much noise)

## Strategy Parameters (Crypto 4H)

```
Consolidation period: At least 5-10 candles
Volume confirmation: 2x 20-period MA
Stop loss: Below breakout level (3-5% depending on volatility)
Take profit: Measured move (range × 1.5) OR next major level
Risk:Reward: Minimum 1:1.5, prefer 1:2
RSI filter: >50 for longs, <50 for shorts
Entry: On retest of broken level (safer) or on breakout (more aggressive)
```
